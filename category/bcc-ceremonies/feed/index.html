Phase I of the planned garden has an anticipated opening of 2019 and will include a colorful natural meadow designed by world renowned Dutchman, Piet Oudolf.

With over 100 people in attendance Governor Markell said “I’m so impressed with what you’ve done here,” and called the garden’s initial development over the past four years “an awesome job.” As he told the garden leaders-including President Susan Ryan, Executive Director Sheryl Swed, and Vice President Ray Sander-“You had a very compelling vision.”

Bancroft will serve as Construction Manager for the project along with with award-winning architecture firm Lake/Flato, Pennoni, and Rodney Robinson of Robinson Anderson Summers.

The 37.5 acre site off of Piney Neck Road in Dagsboro, DE will eventually be developed to include a meadow garden, coastal living garden, special events space, grotto and a boardwalk leading to water access of Pepper Creek.

Visit the Delaware Botanic website to learn more about the future of Delaware Botanic Gardens!
